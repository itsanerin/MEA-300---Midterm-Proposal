Research Site B

One website I found to be useful is Adele's official musician website. Adele happens to be the most successful and popular modern Indie Blues artist. I've decided to draw inspiration from Adele's romantic, dim-colored, black, white, and sepia aesthetic for the theme of my final website, especially the homepage.

The homepage will act primarily as a portal to the other website pages, thereby making it a purely aesthetic page. This is important because an artist's website is representative of their artistic vision and image. By focusing solely on the aesthetic on the home page, the viewers get a vivid sense of Black's artistic vibe right away, hopefully intrigued enough to navigate further to find out more.

Another thing I noticed about Adele's site is that it showcases all the basic elements featured in my Research Site A, as well as a single-page long scroll parallax effect, which has become increasingly popular. I wasn't sure I wanted to implement this setting, for reasons I'll continue to talk about in the page for my Research Site C.
